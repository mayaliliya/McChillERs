Our Mission

BioTRON is a University of Waterloo Design Team that focuses on engineering medical prototypes that improve the lives of real users. We aim to give back to our community by donating all our builds to those in need once they are finished. Students from all programs come together on our team to 3D print prosthetic hands for kids, build exoskeleton hands for people with paralysis, or prototype robotic arms.

Projects

eNABLE

We are the Waterloo chapter of Enabling The Future - a global community that uses their 3D printers to create free upper limb assistive devices for children. Through eNABLE we have certified and donated 3 hands.

NeoWulf

We focus on designing exoskeleton hands that augment the grip and dexterity of people with arm paralysis. Currently we are working with Jesse, a quadriplegic from Illinois, to close his hand when he flexes his bicep.

Powered Arm

With the goal to compete in the Cybathalon, we are driven to design and build a robotic arm prosthetic that will hopefully in the future assist amputees during their everyday tasks.
