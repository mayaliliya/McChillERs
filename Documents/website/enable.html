Our Goal

Our goal is to donate upper arm prosthetic devices to children to make a difference in our world the way we know best! The eNABLE team is the Waterloo chapter for Enabling The Future, a global volunteer organization that connects makers who fabricate and assemble 3D printed hands for recipients who were born with missing fingers or those who have lost them due to war, disease, or natural disaster. We currently use a wide variety of skills for each stage of the process: from design, printing, part sourcing, and assembly.

We take on cases posted on the eNABLE Web Central based on what kind of devices are requested and what devices we are certified for. Once we have received sizing information, we 3D print the parts, perform post processing such as sanding and hole drilling, and then acquire any specialized parts to fully assemble the hand. Once finished we send the completed hand to the child in need. If you or someone you know would like us to make an eNABLE device for you, please contact us as we are happy to help!

The Daniel Project

Daniel is a double upper limb amputee living in El Salvador. In 2018 we started working on a custom prosthetic, and have since 3D printed him a RIT Arm v2 that he received in December. Our next steps are to develop powered modifications that will assist him in writing, to help him in his university studies in psychology.
